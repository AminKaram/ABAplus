ABA+

Assumption-Based Argumentation with Preferences.

This website (www-abaplus.doc.ic.ac.uk) will carry an interface of a system that implements the computational argumentation formalism ABA+ (K. Cyras, F. Toni: ABA+: Assumption-Based Argumentation with Preferences. The 15th International Conference on Principles of Knowledge Representation and Reasoning (KR 2016)). ABA+ is a novel approach to handling preferences in structured argumentation. It is built upon the well established structured argumentation formalism, Assumption-Based Argumentation (ABA), and extends it with a binary preference relation over assumptions. The preferences are incorporated directly into the attack relation and induce a mechanism to reverse attacks. On this website, the users will be able to specify the individual components of an ABA+ framework (i.e. rules, assumptions with their contraries, and preferences among assumptions), and the extensions (under various semantics) will be generated and visualized.

For more information about ABA+ please contact Kristijonas Cyras (k.cyras13@ic.ac.uk) or Francesca Toni (ft@ic.ac.uk). The implementation is carried out by Ziyi Bao (ziyi.bao14@ic.ac.uk), under the Undergraduate Research Opportunities Programme (UROP, http://www.imperial.ac.uk/urop), supervised by K. Cyras and F.
